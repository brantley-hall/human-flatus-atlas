# Human Flatus Atlas Project
## University of Maryland Smart Underwear Research

---

## 🎯 Project Overview

The Human Flatus Atlas is a groundbreaking research project at the University of Maryland that uses smart underwear technology to establish medical baselines for human flatulence patterns. This research aims to revolutionize gastrointestinal health understanding through continuous, non-invasive monitoring.

---

## 🔬 Research Background

### Current Medical Gap
- Physicians lack reliable means to quantify daily gas production
- No established baseline for "normal" flatulence patterns
- Previous studies relied on invasive methods or unreliable self-reports
- Past studies underestimated frequency (14x/day vs. actual 32x/day)

### Technology Innovation
- Smart underwear with electrochemical sensors
- 24/7 continuous monitoring (including during sleep)
- Non-invasive, accurate data collection
- Real-time flatulence tracking

---

## 📊 Key Research Findings

### Baseline Establishment
```
💨 Healthy adults: 32 times/day (average)
📊 Range: 4 times/day (low) to 59 times/day (high)
🔬 Previous estimates: 14 times/day (significant underestimation)
📈 Individual variation: Considerable
```

### Medical Significance
- Establishes first reliable medical baseline
- Enables better diagnosis of gastrointestinal conditions
- Correlates patterns with diet and microbiome
- Improves patient care and treatment
